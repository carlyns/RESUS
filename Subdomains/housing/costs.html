Hypothetical 6-Unit Building

Be sure to consider both Initial and Ongoing Costs!

What are the INITIAL costs?

Initial costs include: property acquisition, repairs/rehab, contingency, apprasal, survey, inspections fees, legal fees, design fees, loan fees, title fees, transfer taxes, and reserves for capital or lease up.

For this hypothetical example, we'll say the Total Project Costs = $440K

Groups often spread the total project costs across the units to derive their full unit values or "unit shares."

In this case of 4 two-bedroom units and 2 one-bedroom units, let's say the group decided that the two-bedroom units have a share value of $80K and the one-bedroom units have a share value of $60K. (or "unit prices")

$80K + $60K + $80K + $80K + $60K + $80K = $440K

Can your group afford to pay for this building in cash? Well, more power to ya! We're assuming that most groups can only pay a portion up front and will finance the remainder.

Some group members can "put down" more upfront, or Member Equity:

$10K + $5K + $30K + $10K + $0K + $30K

= $85K

= your Group Equity (think of it as a collective down payment)

This means your group is putting down 19% of $440K total.

Your group still needs to finance $355k somehow!

So even though these are initial costs, your group and its members will likely be paying these over time.

This usually means your monthly fee will include a contribution to the group loan.

What are the ONGOING costs?

The group shares these ongoing costs.

Groups can decide whether dividing these costs should reflect unit sizes or variations in amenities. There is no standard - it's up to your group!

Groups should also consider potential increases in costs due to:

  • inflation
  • fluctuating property tax rates
  • fluctuating utility costs
  • fluctuating insurance rates
  • etc

We've been working on housing initiatives for a while, and we genuinely think Shared Equity / Property Ownership has potential to provide decent, affordable housing for all.
